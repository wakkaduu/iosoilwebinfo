Category 01 — Arid
Desert-Adapted Plants
Arid plants are masters of water conservation, engineered by nature to thrive in near-dry soil conditions. If you tend to forget watering schedules, these resilient species are your ideal companions—they reward neglect far more than overwatering.
Ideal soil moisture range
10% – 20%
Watering Logic
Trigger Water when soil moisture drops below 10%, typically every 14–21 days in growing season. Always verify with a soil probe; visual cues alone are unreliable.
Threshold Stop watering once moisture reaches 20%. Never allow substrate to stay saturated. Drain excess immediately—standing water is lethal to most arid species.
Growing Conditions
Soil Mix
50% coarse sand + 30% perlite + 20% potting mix. Gritty, ultra-draining substrate is essential.
Light
Full sun, 6–8+ hrs daily. South or west-facing exposure preferred. Avoid filtered shade.
Humidity
10–30% RH. Low ambient humidity required. Avoid humid bathrooms or enclosed terrariums.

Category 02 — Drought-Tolerant
Resilient Water-Efficient Plants
Drought-tolerant plants occupy the sweet spot between arid cacti and thirsty tropicals, storing moisture in their leaves, stems, or roots for dry periods. They're excellent choices for water-conscious gardeners who still want lush, varied greenery without constant irrigation demands.
Ideal soil moisture range
20% – 40%
Watering Logic
Trigger Water when top 2–3 cm of soil is fully dry and moisture reads below 20%. Frequency: every 7–14 days in summer, monthly in winter dormancy.
Threshold Stop at 40% moisture. Allow full drainage before returning pots to saucers. Moderate wet-dry cycles mimic natural steppe environments and promote root health.
Growing Conditions
Soil Mix
Well-draining loam: 40% potting soil + 30% perlite + 30% grit or pumice for drainage.
Light
Bright indirect to full sun, 4–8 hrs. Tolerates partial shade but reduces overall vigour.
Humidity
30–50% RH. Tolerant of dry indoor air. Misting is unnecessary and often counterproductive.

Category 03 — Tropical
High-Humidity Rainforest Plants
Tropical plants evolved in environments of abundant moisture and dappled light, thriving when their soil stays consistently moist but never waterlogged. Consistent humidity and warm temperatures are just as important as watering frequency for keeping these lush species healthy indoors.
Ideal soil moisture range
50% – 70%
Watering Logic
Trigger Water when sensor reads below 50% or top inch of soil feels dry. In active growth (spring–summer), check every 3–5 days. Reduce frequency in winter.
Threshold Stop at 70% moisture. Ensure pots have drainage holes. Root rot develops rapidly above 80%—aeration of the root zone is critical alongside moisture retention.
Growing Conditions
Soil Mix
Peat-free loam: 60% coco coir + 20% perlite + 20% orchid bark for aeration and structure.
Light
Bright indirect light, 4–6 hrs. No harsh afternoon sun. East-facing windows are ideal.
Humidity
60–80% RH. Use a pebble tray, humidifier, or group plants to raise ambient humidity.

Category 04 — Leafy Vegetable
Fast-Growing Edible Crops
Leafy vegetables are productive, fast-cycling crops that reward attentive and consistent watering with tender, flavourful harvests. Because most of their edible mass is water, allowing the soil to dry out—even briefly—can trigger bolting, bitterness, and wilting that reduces yield quality.
Ideal soil moisture range
55% – 75%
Watering Logic
Trigger Irrigate when moisture drops to 55%. In hot weather or grow-light setups, check daily. Consistent moisture prevents bolting, tipburn (calcium deficiency), and bitter flavour compounds.
Threshold Maintain at or below 75% moisture. For outdoor beds, deep infrequent watering (drip irrigation) encourages deep root growth vs. shallow surface watering.
Growing Conditions
Soil Mix
Rich loamy soil: 50% compost + 30% topsoil + 20% perlite. High nitrogen content essential.
Light
Full sun to partial shade, 4–8 hrs. Shade cloth recommended above 35°C to prevent bolting.
Humidity
50–70% RH. Good air circulation reduces fungal issues such as downy mildew and grey mould.

Category 05 — Aquatic
Submerged & Marginal Water Plants
Aquatic plants are uniquely adapted to saturated or fully submerged substrates, requiring near-constant water presence to survive and thrive. Unlike every other plant category, the primary management challenge is not overwatering but ensuring adequate water chemistry, dissolved oxygen, and nutrient levels.
Ideal soil moisture range
85% – 100%+
Watering Logic
Trigger Maintain water level consistently. For marginal species, refill when water drops below 85% substrate saturation. For submerged species, maintain continuous coverage of 2–15 cm above crown.
Threshold No upper moisture limit applies. Monitor pH (6.5–8.5), dKH (3–8), and EC/TDS rather than moisture. Replace 10–20% of water weekly to prevent nutrient stagnation and algae blooms.
Growing Conditions
Substrate
Aquatic clay or loam topped with coarse gravel. Avoid potting mix—it clouds water and decomposes rapidly.
Light
Full sun to bright indirect, 4–8 hrs. Floating species naturally shade submerged ones below.
Water Quality
Temp 15–28°C. Oxygenate via aerator or fountain pump. Test pH weekly. Remove algae promptly.
